PREFACE

Spontaneous development of the human civilization has been accompanied by a continuous increase of impact of environment, and first of all on live nature In the twentieth century this pressure exceeded the admissible level, destruction of natural ecosystems and environment pollution acquired such a scale that they have started to clearly affect indicators of global environmental characteristics, and particularly the rate of changing these indicators Along with such anthropogenic processes as shifts in the atmosphere chemical content (increase in concentration of greenhouse gases) and thinning of the ozone stratum, rather hazardous tendencies also include desertification, downsizing of forests and biological diversity.

Pressure of the civilization on the live nature has led to exclusively high (unprecedented in the history of the Earth) rate of extinction of species (and subspecies) of animals, plants and fungi The hazard of biological diversity downsizing is often viewed from the economic point of view (downsizing of the genetic pool decreases the opportunities in the area of biotechnology development), based on ethic, esthetic, social and pedagogical principles These are very important aspects, but a more important aspect is that biodiversity downsizing is a sign of biota degradation, hazard of destruction of natural mechanisms for environment stabilization, destruction of the environmental balance, neanng of the environmental catastrophe.

Preservation of the genetic pool of live creatures, and first of all endangered species (i e the part of biota that is the most vulnerable from the point of view of biological diversity) is one of the mam tasks of environment protection In accordance with current legislation, rare and endangered species of animals, plants and fungi are included in the Red Data Books of the Russian Federation and of the Subjects of the Russian Federation Inclusion in the Red Data Book is a legal act (formalizing sign) imposing constraints on relevant species as objects of legal protection from other representatives of flora and fauna The organizational and legal guaranties increasing opportunities in the area of conservation and rehabilitation are effective in relation to the species included in the Red Data Book.

Maintenance of the Red Data Book of the Russian Federation is a continuous work on monitoring of rare and endangered species and on implementation of the above organizational and legal guarantees The present edition summarizes this work for the last fifteen years after its first edition

The role of the Red Data Book of the Russian Federation as a popular scientific reference-book is significant It includes data on those aspects of biology of rare and endangered species that reflect causes of their rareness and extinction, constraints that substantiate these phenomena, areas of activities that facilitate conservation of these species I would like also to note that conservation of rare and endangered species (and equally those species that fortunately are not currently endangered) is possible only in case of preservation, rehabilitation, expansion of those ecosystems where they live, necessary elements of which they are and to which their genetic programs are oriented.

The proposed edition is a result of joint work of many scientists and highly-qualified specialists of central and regional scientific institutions, higher educational institutes, legislative and executive authorities I am sure that this work is necessary for preservation of nature of Russia.

The Red Data Book of the Russian Federation includes rare and endangered animals, plants and fungi that permanently or temporarily live under conditions of natural freedom at the territory, continental shelf and in the marine economic zone of the Russian Federation and that need special state legal activities within competence of federal executive authorities The Red Data Book of the Russian Federation is meeting the obligation of the Russian Federation assumed in accordance with the Convention on Biological Diversity approved in 1992 in Rio-de-Janeiro.

The Red Data Book of the Russian Federation that includes fauna objects (the Red Data Book of RSFSR) was for the first time published in 1983 The Department of Hunting and Protected Areas under the Council of Ministers of the Russian Federation (Glavokhota RSFSR) prepared and published this edition The Central Hunting and Protected Areas Scientific and Research Laboratory of Glavokhota RSFSR (CNIL Glavokhota RSFSR, at present State Agency "Control Information and Analytical Center for Hunted Animals and Their Habitats" of the Ministry of Agriculture and Food Supplies of Russia) with attraction of zoologists of Russian academic and other leading research institutions provided scientific support.

In accordance with the applicable civil laws, the Red Data Book should be published at least once per ten years Compilation and agreement of the lists of fauna objects to be included in the Red Data Book were continued practically right after publication of the Red Data Book of RSFSR in 1983 CNIL Glavokhota RSFSR collected a considerable part of materials for preparation of essays on species for the Red Data Book Further developments in the country introduced significant changes in the commenced work The preparation of the final version of the Red Data Book of the Russian Federation and its publication were realized by A N Severtsov Institute of Ecology and Evolution of the Russian Academy of Sciences.

After establishing a system of environmental agencies in 1988, the function for maintenance of the Red Data Book of the Russian Federation was transferred to the Ministry of Nature of Russia The Russian Academy of Sciences and the Russian Scientific and Research Institute of Nature Protection realize the provision of scientific support for it In 1992 the Commission for Rare and Endangered Animals, Plants and Fungi was established under the Ministry of Nature of Russia, the Commission comprises special sections and working groups of experts

In 1994 the Commission considered results of the work done and recommended to the Ministry of Nature of Russia to commence formal procedures of agreement with administrations of subjects of the Russian Federation and interested federal executive agencies of the list of fauna objects to be included in the Red Data Book and three proposals that are not covered by the jurisdiction of the Red Data Book Agreement of the list with over 90 subjects of the Russian Federation and federal ministries and agencies took approximately two years In parallel, work on development of regulatory legal acts ensuring maintenance of the Red Data Book, monitoring and protection of objects included in it, was done.

It was taken into consideration that the national Red Data Book includes objects requiring special federal-level protection measures These measures must ensure inter aha control and meeting of international obligations of Russia for the conservation of these objects, coordination of state measures between subjects of the Russian Federation and state control over conservation of an object living at the territory of only one subject of the Russian Federation if its extinction at this territory results in loss of this object from fauna of Russia

The Red Data Book of the Russian Federation includes six categories of rareness of taxons and populations depending on the hazard of their extinction 0 - probably extinct, 1 - endangered, 2 - decreasing in number, 3 - rare, 4 - status undetermined, 5 - rehabilitating and being rehabilitated.

The "probably extinct" category includes taxons and populations about which there is information that they lived at the territory (aquatory) of the Russian Federation, but which presence in nature has not been confirmed for invertebrates - for the last 100 years, for vertebrates - for the last 50 years The "endangered" category unites taxons and populations which number has decreased to a critical level so that in the near future they can become extinct The "decreasing in number" category includes taxons and populations which number is steadily decreasing and that may in the shortest time period join the "endangered" category The "rare" category includes taxons and populations that are small in number and/or spread at a limited territory (aquatory) or sporadically spread at significant territories (aquatories) The "undetermined status" category includes taxons and populations that require special protection measures but in relation to which there are currently no sufficient data or available data do not fully comply with criteria of all other categories The "rehabilitating and being rehabilitated" category includes taxons and populations the number and spreading of which under influence of natural causes or in the result of protection measures taken have begun to rehabilitate and are nearing the condition when they will not need prompt measures aimed at protection and rehabilitation.

Taking into consideration diversity of fauna and the scale of the territory of Russia, the revision of the environmental status and fauna representatives rarity categories was carried out on the basis of consultations with specialists of over 70 institutions of the Russian Academy of Sciences and other leading central and regional research and higher educational institutions The large number and extremely poor coverage of studies of species of invertebrates, practical difficulty in their identification by employees of environmental agencies predetermined the possibility of including into the Red Data Book only forms related to the first three categories, and first of all largest and most popular species that often become objects of excessive interest of collectors In addition to the mam section (that includes essays on condition of species of animals included in the Red Data Book of the Russian Federation, the essays are arranged in a systematic order), the Red Data Book includes three appendixes:

  1. The annotated list of taxons and populations excluded from the Red Data Book of the Russian Federation It includes taxons and populations, data on rehabilitation of the number and (or) natural habitats of which, as well as on positive changes in conditions of their existence, confirm that there is no necessity to take prompt measures aimed at their protection and reproduction
  2. The annotated list of taxons and populations of the world fauna that became extinct in the Russian Federation It includes those animals about which there is information that they lived at the territory (aquatory) of Russia beginning from the middle of the eighteenth century, but data on existence of which have not been received for the last 100 years (for invertebrates) and 50 years (for vertebrates)
  3. The annotated list of taxons and populations requiring special attention to their state in natural environment They include those taxons and populations, data on number and ranges of which, as well as on conditions of their existence, confirm that at present there is no need to take special measures aimed at their protection and reproduction at the federal level, but because of limited distribution or other biological peculiarities these taxons and populations are vulnerable and these measures may be necessary This list also includes taxons and populations included in the IUCN Red List, Red Lists of Europe and Asia, the state of which at the territory (aquatory) of Russia don't require special measures aimed at their protection

In contrast to the mam section, the above appendixes are not legal documents and their objective is to attract attention of environmental and scientific community to state of populations of species of animals listed in them
